Class Contraindication

  • Parent class: Observable
  • A concern about the performance of an action (proposed, ongoing, or past), e.g., medication intake, due to a health reason. A contraindication is a specific situation in which a drug, procedure, or surgery should not be used because it may be harmful to the patient. e.g., Contraindication to gentamycin due to poor kidney function.

    • Field Summary

      Fields 
      Field Type and Description
      contraindicatedAct Act
      The action that is to be withheld in the context of the contraindication. Note that a contraindication may apply to the administration of a substance or to the performance of a procedure, for instance.
      degree Code
      May be absolute or relative. An absolute contraindication means that the course of action MUST be avoided. A relative contraindication means that the course of action SHOULD be avoided but that the risk of proceeding with the course of action may be outweighed by other factors or mitigated in some way.
      effectiveTime TimePeriod
      Time period during which the contraindication holds. This may be an open interval if no end time is currently known.
      inference Inference
      Justification or reason for withholding treatment.
